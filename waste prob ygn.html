Waste Problems in Yangon

Yangon is facing a growing waste crisis as urban development increases faster than waste management systems can handle.

This affects people, health, and the environment every day.

2,500+ Tons Daily



A Local Crisis Growing Every Day

Yangon generates over 2,500 tons of waste every day. As the city grows rapidly, waste systems struggle to keep up. Overflowing bins, plastic pollution, and unmanaged waste are becoming major urban challenges.

13%

of Yangon’s waste is plastic pollution

Main Waste Problems

Overflowing Bins & Collection Gaps

Communal bins overflow into streets, attracting pests and blocking drainage systems.

Many people dump waste informally because collection points are too far.

Plastic Pollution

Plastic waste blocks waterways and causes flooding during monsoon seasons.

Microplastics contaminate water, soil, and food chains.

Poor Waste Separation

Organic, recyclable, and hazardous waste are mixed together.

This reduces recycling opportunities and increases landfill pressure.

Open Burning of Waste

People burn waste when collection services fail.

Burning plastic releases toxic chemicals harmful to health.

How Waste Becomes a Bigger Problem

More Waste

Population growth creates more trash every day.

Poor Collection

Systems cannot collect waste fast enough.

Pollution

Plastic, smoke, and chemicals spread into the environment.

Health Risks

People suffer from diseases and poor living conditions.

Effects on People

  • Asthma and breathing problems
  • Skin irritation
  • Waterborne diseases
  • Unsafe neighborhoods

Effects on Environment

  • Groundwater pollution
  • Flooding from blocked drains
  • Soil contamination
  • Loss of clean ecosystems
